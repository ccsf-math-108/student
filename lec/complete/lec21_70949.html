Benford's Law¶

Benford's law, also known as the Newcomb-Benford law, the law of anomalous numbers, or the first-digit law, is an observation that in many real-life sets of numerical data, the leading digit is likely to be small. Benford's law tends to apply most accurately to data that span several orders of magnitude. As a rule of thumb, the more orders of magnitude that the data evenly covers, the more accurately Benford's law applies.

Alternative hypothesis: The difference in the two distributions is not due to chance.

def tvd(dist1, dist2):
    return sum(abs(dist1 - dist2)) / 2

Are the data consistent with the null hypothesis?

Yes! With a p-value much larger than 5%, we fail to reject the null hypothesis. We don't have evidence to say Benford is wrong!

def compute_test_statistic(tbl):
    grouped = tbl.group('Bachelor Degree', np.average)
    avgs = grouped.column('Reaction Time average')
    return abs(avgs.item(1) - avgs.item(0))

def simulate_under_null():
